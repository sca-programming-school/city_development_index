About the City Development Index

The City Development Index (CDI) is a measure designed to capture the level of development in cities. It is calculated using a variety of indicators, including infrastructure, social services, economic status, and environmental sustainability. The index helps to provide a comprehensive overview of the quality of life and development in different urban areas.

The CDI is calculated by aggregating various sub-indices that represent different dimensions of urban development. These sub-indices typically include measures of housing, healthcare, education, income, and employment, among others. The final index value is a weighted average of these sub-indices, providing a single composite score that reflects the overall development status of the city.
